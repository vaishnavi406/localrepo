What is Cryptocurrency?

Definition: Cryptocurrencies are digital assets designed to work as a medium of exchange using strong cryptography to secure transactions, control the creation of additional units, and verify the transfer of assets. Characteristics:

  1. Digital Nature: No physical form, exists only online.
  2. Global Accessibility: Transactions can occur across borders without traditional financial intermediaries.
  3. Limited Supply: Most cryptocurrencies have a capped supply (e.g., Bitcoin is capped at 21 million coins).
  4. Trustless System: Transactions are verified through a network c onsensus mechanism without needing intermediaries like banks.
